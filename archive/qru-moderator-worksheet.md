## Session Design

**Format:** 45-minute moderated peer discussion (not a panel, not a pitch)
**Energy:** Conversational, candid, off-the-record feel
**Goal:** Surface real tactical insights. Everyone participates. Best discussion of the day.

---

## Roles

**Ashley — The Driver**
- Delivers the opening frame
- Asks the main questions for each round
- Manages time and transitions
- Closes the session

**Cliff — The Sharpener**
- Asks follow-up questions that go deeper
- Pulls quiet people into the conversation by name
- Adds cross-brand pattern observations when natural ("We see this a lot across our portfolio...")
- Keeps energy up if conversation stalls

**Rule for both:** Ask questions > make statements. The smartest people in the room ask the best questions.

---

## Opening Frame — Ashley (60–90 seconds)

> "Thanks everyone for being here. Before we start, quick context on how we built this session. Qru shared anonymized responses from this group ahead of time, and a few things jumped out.
>
> Almost everyone in this room is navigating the same tension: you started as a challenger brand — opinionated, fast, willing to make bets — and now the organization around you is asking for certainty, efficiency, and proof. The scrappy thing that made you dangerous is getting harder to protect.
>
> So that's what we want to dig into. Not theory — what's actually working inside your organizations right now, and where you're feeling real pressure. This is a conversation, not a presentation. Jump in, disagree, build on what someone else says."

---

## Round 1 — The Trade-Off Question (15 min)

**Ashley asks:**

> "Let's start with something concrete. When resources get constrained — budget cuts, hiring freeze, board pressure — what do you protect first, and what gets sacrificed?"

**Cliff follow-ups (use 1-2, not all):**

- → *"Matt, you've talked about cutting brand spend to protect lower-funnel acquisition. Looking back, was that the right call?"*
- → *"Lauren, you took the opposite approach — invested in foundational systems when the pressure was to chase visible wins. How did you sell that internally?"*
- → *"Danny, you narrowed distribution instead of expanding. How did your board react to that?"*
- → *"Rico, you mentioned cutting anything beyond a 12-month horizon. Where do you draw that line — is 6 months safe? 3?"*

**What to listen for:** Are people protecting brand or performance? Systems or campaigns? This reveals their real priorities.

---

## Round 2 — The Proof Problem (15 min)

**Ashley asks:**

> "A theme that came up repeatedly is measurement — specifically, proving that the things you're protecting actually compound over time. What does 'proof' look like for brand or long-term investment inside your org?"

**Cliff follow-ups:**

- → *"Shelby, you mentioned new subscriber growth and CAC under constant scrutiny. What would give you permission to take a bigger brand swing?"*
- → *"Is anyone here using incrementality testing or media mix modeling to justify upper-funnel spend? Or is it still gut and faith?"*
- → *"Diane, you sit across both brand and performance at Cocolab — how do you talk about those two things to the same leadership team?"*
- → *"For the founders in the room — Craig, Matt, Rico — is the proof problem different when you're the one making the call versus selling it up?"*

**What to listen for:** The gap between what they *know* works and what they can *prove* works. That gap is where frustration lives.

---

## Round 3 — The Content & Creator Engine (10 min)

**Ashley asks:**

> "Let's shift to something tactical. The volume problem keeps coming up — algorithms want more content than most teams can produce. How are you solving the production problem?"

**Cliff follow-ups:**

- → *"Matt, you raised the creator compensation question — gifting, flat fees, affiliate, equity. What's actually working right now?"*
- → *"Is anyone treating creators as production partners rather than just distribution? Meaning they're making your ads, not just posting about you?"*
- → *"Andrew, at Olaplex's scale, how do you maintain brand consistency when you're sourcing content from dozens of creators?"*
- → *"Sara, Nanit has such a specific audience — how do you find creators who actually understand the parent experience vs. just the aesthetic?"*

**What to listen for:** Who has a system vs. who is winging it. The brands with real creator production systems are ahead.

---

## Closing — Ashley (3 min)

> "We're coming up on time, so I want to close with one question. Go around quickly:"

> **"What's one thing from this conversation you're going to think about differently next week?"**

*(Let everyone answer. Don't rush. This is where the best insights land.)*

> "Thank you all — this was exactly the kind of conversation we were hoping for. We'll be around for the cocktail party, so grab us if you want to keep going on any of this."

---

## Emergency Prompts (If Conversation Stalls)

Only use these if there's a dead moment:

- "Does anyone want to push back on that?"
- "Who's had the opposite experience?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your team operates, what would it be?"
- "What's the conversation you have internally that you'd never say on a panel?"

---

## Participation Management

☐ If someone hasn't spoken by Round 2, pull them in by name: *"Sara, I'd love your take on this"*
☐ If someone is dominating, redirect: *"Great point — who else is experiencing something similar?"*
☐ If the conversation goes deep on a tangent that's working, let it run — don't force the next question
☐ If someone gives a surface-level answer, Cliff goes deeper: *"Can you unpack that? What did that actually look like day to day?"*

## Moderator Reminders

✅ You are moderators first, TT second
✅ Ask fewer questions — go deeper on each one
✅ Let participants do 80% of the talking
✅ Use their names and reference their pre-survey responses — it shows you did your homework
✅ Never pitch. If someone asks what TT does, keep it to one sentence: *"We're a performance marketing agency — we sit behind the scenes running growth engines for brands like the ones in this room"*
✅ The best outcome is people saying "that was the best breakout" — not "Tiger Tracks seems cool"
