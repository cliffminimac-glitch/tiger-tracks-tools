SECTION 7: POUNCE V3 — FULL DETAIL
================================================================================

What Pounce Does:
Pounce is Tiger Tracks' internal AI-powered business development outreach tool.
It automates the entire cold outreach workflow from lead discovery through email
delivery and reply tracking. Ashley is the sole operator. Reps (Alex, Steven) 
never touch it directly — they only see HubSpot when a meeting is booked.

Live URL: https://pounce-v3-three.vercel.app
Password: tigertracks2026

Technology stack:
- Frontend/backend: Next.js (React, TypeScript) on Vercel
- Database: Supabase (PostgreSQL)
- Email sending: Gmail service account with domain-wide delegation
  (sends as reps from trytigertracks.ai domain)
- Contact sourcing: Apollo.io API
- Email verification: MillionVerifier API
- Sequence generation: Anthropic Claude (claude-sonnet / claude-opus)
- CRM: HubSpot (deals created on meeting booked)
- Reply detection: Gmail API (not Instantly)
- Slack notifications: Slack webhook (not bot token)

Why trytigertracks.ai, not tigertracks.ai:
Sending from a secondary domain protects the primary domain's deliverability.
The trytigertracks.ai accounts were warmed for 30+ days before use.

The 3 reps and their Pounce industry verticals (rotate weekly):
- Alex: Beauty & Personal Care, Healthcare, Home Services, Consumer Goods
- Steven: Food & Beverage, Apparel & Fashion, Entertainment & Toys, Real Estate
- Ashley: Health & Wellness, E-Commerce & DTC, Financial Services, SaaS & Technology

Weekly workflow (Monday morning):
1. Apollo pull runs automatically at 7AM UTC (Monday cron)
2. Pull fetches 25 contacts/rep = 75 total (industry rotation determines verticals)
3. MillionVerifier checks all emails before importing
4. Valid contacts → Supabase → pipeline triggers Claude sequence generation
5. Auto-approve quality gate checks each sequence (banned phrases, no fake stats,
   opener length); passing sequences auto-approved; failing ones stay as draft
6. Ashley reviews Sequences page (takes ~10-15 min)
7. Kills obvious misfits, approves borderline cases
8. Cron at 8AM ET (Monday-Friday) sends scheduled emails via Gmail service account

Sequence structure (6 emails per contact):
- Email 1: insight-first opener, no mention of Tiger Tracks capabilities
- Email 2: industry context, second angle
- Email 3-4: proof/social proof, CTA to exploratory call
- Email 5: soft break-up, different angle
- Email 6: final follow-up
- Tone rules: NEVER criticize the prospect, NEVER state what they're missing,
  lead with TT's industry expertise and genuine curiosity about the prospect
- No fabricated stats allowed (absolute rule, enforced by prompt and post-processor)
- No "At Tiger Tracks we..." as an opener
- No em dashes anywhere

Quality gate (auto-approve logic):
Sequences that pass all 3 checks are auto-approved:
1. No banned phrases ("brands like yours", "caught my eye", "impressive presence",
   "At Tiger Tracks we..." as opener)
2. No unapproved statistics (no specific numbers or percentages not from case studies)
3. Opener ≤ 6 sentences
Failing sequences stay as draft for Ashley's manual review.

Case studies used in sequences:
- AG1: universal fallback, supplement/health
- Rho Nutrition: performance nutrition, 5.39 ROAS vs 1.78
- Anastasia Beverly Hills: beauty/cosmetics
- Capezio: apparel/dance
- SPS Commerce: supply chain/retail
- Dovetail Furniture: home goods/DTC
- Leonard Truck Outfitters: consumer goods/accessories

Apollo pull details:
- Scheduled: Mondays 7AM UTC (Vercel cron)
- Volume: 25 contacts per rep per week (75 total)
- Industry filter uses lowercase sub-vertical names (Apollo API is case-sensitive)
- Email status filter removed (Apollo plan doesn't support it; MillionVerifier handles verification)
- Contacts with invalid emails go to DNC list automatically
- Dedup guard: won't pull again within 6 days (can be overridden with force:true)

Email send cron:
- Scheduled: 8AM ET, Monday-Friday (1PM UTC)
- Sends any email_schedule rows with sent_at IS NULL and scheduled_for <= NOW()
- Embeds tracking pixel in each email for open detection
- Bounce detection: checks for MAILER-DAEMON in rep Gmail inbox before step 2+
- On bounce: sequence marked bounced, email added to DNC, Slack alert sent

Replies:
- Pounce scans Gmail for replies on threads it owns
- Replies surface in the Replies tab with sentiment classification
- "Draft Reply" button: Claude generates a context-aware reply for Ashley to review
  and send herself (she pastes it into Gmail)
- "Mark Meeting Booked" button: sets sequence to meeting_booked status, generates
  pre-call brief, creates/updates HubSpot deal

HubSpot integration:
- On positive reply: sequence → Engaging, HubSpot contact/deal updated
- On meeting booked: sequence → meeting_booked, pre-call brief generated by Claude
  and pushed as a note on the HubSpot deal
- Ashley provides her own HubSpot token

Pounce page structure:
Main nav: Dashboard → Deal Board → Campaigns → Sequences → Replies
Admin nav: Lead Finder → ProspectBase → Case Studies → The Vault → Intelligence
           → Best Practices → User Manual → Settings

Dashboard shows: Revenue Forecast, Outreach Funnel (Enrolled → Sent → Opened
→ Replied → Meetings → Closed), Hot Leads, Sender Health, Weekly Briefing

Campaigns page shows: Team summary (Sent/Open%/Reply%/Meetings/Bounce%),
Rep scorecards (Alex/Steven/Ashley), Vertical breakdown, Goal tracking

Key Pounce decisions made along the way:
- Gmail over Instantly: Instantly has API limitations (can't set sending account
  via API), and Gmail service account is more controllable and cost-effective.
  Instantly is kept only for domain health monitoring.
- Claude over GPT-4o: Better instruction following, less hallucination, better
  tone calibration for professional B2B outreach.
- 25/rep/week (not 67): Volume was increased from the initial goal, then pulled
  back to 25/rep after quality concerns. Better to have 75 high-quality sequences
  than 200 mediocre ones.
- Auto-approve quality gate: Added after noticing manual review was a bottleneck.
  Auto-approves sequences that pass all quality checks, keeping Ashley's review
  to ~10-15 minutes of borderline cases per week.
- No A/B testing yet: Not enough volume (need 200+ sends per variant for
  statistical significance). Revisit at 500+ sends/month.
- Industry vertical rotation: Each rep targets different verticals each week via
  a deterministic rotation (week number mod number of verticals). Prevents
  targeting the same industries repeatedly.
- trytigertracks.ai sending domain: Keeps primary tigertracks.ai domain clean.
  Accounts were warmed 30+ days before use.
